Jon Barron

I'm a research scientist at Apple MLR in San Francisco, where I lead a small team that focuses on generative modeling research. At Apple I've worked on research on generative modeling methodologies for images, 3D, graphs and scientific problems. I did my PhD at University of Barcelona, where I was advised by Sergio Escalera. I spent a big part of my PhD at CMU working with Fernando De la Torre on matrix factorization methods. I did my postdoc training with Bjoern Ommer working on unsupervised deep learning and generative models.

Research

I'm interested in scalable and efficient generative modeling across modalities. My long term goal is to unify training recipes and architectures across different data domains. My research style is to focus on simplifying complex pipelines and design decisions and finding what are the key designs that makes things work. Some papers are highlighted.
